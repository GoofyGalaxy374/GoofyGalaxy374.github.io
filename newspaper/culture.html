THE PETRIFIED FOREST

The Petrified Forest is also called the Tree Valley, or Gabaz Dere. It is located near the village of Tatul. The three-dozen or so trees, which have survived to the present day, bear witness to the last thirty million years of earth's history. The stone pillars range from half a meter to three meters in height, and no more than one meter in diameter. Geologists connect this phenomeonon with underwater volcanic activity that created the East Rhodope mountains. According to certain academics, they are the remains of a forest on the shore of a prehistoric sea. According to others, the trunks were formed from the precipitation of dense fine sand which gradually formed pillars resembling tree trunks.
