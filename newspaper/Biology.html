The roots of phobias

Scientists have longly thought that memories and skills are built after we are born but a recent study doesn't say so. according to researchers at the Emory University School Of Medicine mice can pass on learned information about bad experiences- in this case a fear of the smell of cherry blossom - to the next generation. These results may help explain why many people suffer from irrational phobias like snakes, spiders(as i do...) and heights.
